# Chapter 5: The Outreach Machine

---

The content flywheel brings people to you. The outreach machine takes you to them.

This chapter builds a systematic outreach system -- ICP definition, prospect research, email infrastructure, and the 3-email cold sequence that gets replies.

---

## Defining Your ICP

ICP stands for Ideal Customer Profile. It is the specific type of person most likely to buy, most quickly, with the least friction.

### The 5 Personas

**Tier 1: Vibe Coders (Age 19-30)**
- Pain: "Every agent framework requires a third-party dependency I do not trust"
- Trigger: Working demo in under 5 minutes + no framework dependency
- Where: GitHub, YouTube, TikTok
- Approach: Show, do not tell. Demos and code examples.

**Tier 2: Senior Developers (Age 30-42)**
- Pain: "The popular framework has 280K stars but the creator left. What can I trust?"
- Trigger: Clean architecture + protective open source license
- Where: HackerNews, GitHub Issues, technical newsletters
- Approach: Architecture comparisons, security analysis.

**Tier 3: Solopreneurs (Age 25-40)**
- Pain: "Managed AI services cost 8K/year. I want the same capability cheaper."
- Trigger: Cost comparison showing 10x savings with self-hosted
- Where: Indie Hackers, Product Hunt, X/Twitter
- Approach: Revenue stories, cost breakdowns.

**Tier 4: CTOs at Sub-50 Companies**
- Pain: "We need GDPR compliance and an audit trail"
- Trigger: Compliance story, case study
- Where: LinkedIn, conferences
- Approach: Compliance-first messaging, white papers.

**Tier 5: Romanian/CEE Developers (140K-192K pool)**
- Pain: "No localized agent runtime exists"
- Trigger: Romanian-language templates, local pricing
- Where: Facebook Groups, meetups
- Approach: Local content, community events.

Start with Tier 1 or Tier 3. Master one before expanding.

## Email Infrastructure

**Step 1:** Buy a separate domain for outreach. Never use your primary domain.

**Step 2:** Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. All three are non-negotiable.

**Step 3:** Domain warming: 5-10 emails per day for the first 2 weeks, then gradually increase.


## The 3-Email Cold Sequence

### Email 1: The Hook (Day 1)

**Subject:** Quick question about [specific thing they do]

Hi [First Name],

I noticed [specific observation about their work -- a tweet, a repo, a blog post]. [One sentence about why it caught your attention.]

We built [brief description] that [specific benefit relevant to their situation].

Would it be useful if I shared [specific resource -- a case study, a template, a demo]?

Best,
[Your name]

### Email 2: The Proof (Day 3)

**Subject:** Re: Quick question about [specific thing they do]

Hi [First Name],

Wanted to follow up with something concrete.

"We reduced our agent API costs from 300/month to 47/month while generating 290/month in playbook sales. Net positive in 6 weeks."

Here is the breakdown: [link to case study]

No pressure -- just thought it might be relevant.

Best,
[Your name]

### Email 3: The Ask (Day 7)

**Subject:** Re: Quick question about [specific thing they do]

Hi [First Name],

Last follow-up. If the timing is not right, totally understand. But if [specific pain point] is something you are dealing with, I would love to show you a 5-minute demo.

Calendar link: [scheduling link]

Either way, enjoyed seeing your work on [specific reference].

Best,
[Your name]

> **"The 3-email sequence is not about selling. It is about starting a conversation with someone who has a problem you can solve."**

## LinkedIn Outreach

**Step 1: Connect** with a personalized note (under 300 characters).
**Step 2: Engage first.** Like and comment on 2-3 of their posts before messaging.
**Step 3: Message** only after they accept and you have engaged.
**Step 4: Follow up** only if they respond. LinkedIn is a conversation platform.

---

## Metrics That Matter

**Email targets:**
- Open rate: 40-60%
- Reply rate: 5-15%

**LinkedIn targets:**
- Acceptance rate: 30-50%
- Reply rate: 15-30%

If open rates are below 30%, fix your subject lines.
If reply rates are below 3%, your emails are too generic.

> **"Outreach is a diagnostic tool. Low reply rates mean your message or targeting is wrong."**
